📝 Why Documentation Matters

Legal: Chain of custody, evidence preservation for potential prosecution
Operational: Root cause analysis, detection gap identification
Strategic: Executive reporting, ROI for security investments
Tactical: Update runbooks, tune SIEM rules to close coverage gaps

📄 Report Structure (7 Sections)

1. Executive Summary — 1 page, non-technical, business impact
2. Incident Timeline — chronological events to closure
3. Attack Chain — MITRE ATT&CK kill chain mapping
4. Technical Details — SPL evidence, log excerpts
5. Affected Assets & Impact
6. Containment & Remediation
7. Lessons Learned & Recommendations

⚠
Use Response Notes in Splunk ES — NOT Audit Notes. They are permanent, cannot be deleted, and form a tamper-proof audit trail.

SPL · 30-Day Executive Report Statistics index=notable · -30d
index=notable earliest=-30d | stats count as total, sum(eval(if(urgency="critical",1,0))) as critical, dc(src_user) as affected_users, sum(eval(if(status="resolved",1,0))) as resolved | eval resolution_rate = round(resolved/total*100,1)+"%"
📝 Report Checklist ▾
Section 1 — Executive Summary: 1 paragraph, non-technical, business impact focus
Section 2 — Timeline: every event with exact timestamps, source to closure
Section 3 — Root Cause: what vulnerability or gap allowed this to happen?
Section 4 — IOC List: all IPs, domains, hashes, users, hosts
Section 5 — MITRE Mapping: map every technique to ATT&CK IDs
Section 6 — Recommendations: minimum 3 actionable improvements
✓ Lessons Learned Session ▾
What detection gaps existed? Why wasn't it caught earlier?
Was the Runbook sufficient? What should be added?
Do SIEM correlation rules need updates based on new TTPs?
What was MTTR? How can it be improved?
